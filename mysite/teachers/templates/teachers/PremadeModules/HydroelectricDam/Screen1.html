How Does A Hydroelectric Dam Work?

All About Potential Energy:

POTENTIAL energy is the energy an object has because of its position. It can also be called stored energy - energy that an object has, as an inherent characteristic, but not in use. Sometimes, it can be called gravitational potential energy.

PE = mass X g X height

PE = potential energy (joules - J)
g = acceleration due to gravity at sea level (9.81 m/s^2)

Calculate Potential Energy:

PE = mass * g * height

Result: 0.00 Joules

What is Force?

FORCE uses units called Newtons (N). Downward force, or weight, can be found by multiplying mass times gravity.

F = mass X gravity

Gravity = 9.81 m/s^2
A 10 kg object has a downward force of 98.1 N

Calculate Force:

Force = mass * 9.81 m/s^2

Result: 0.00 Newtons

What is Work?

WORK is force acting over a distance. Work uses the unit - Joule (J) or Newton meter.

W = force X distance

Example:

If a force of 10N is used to move an object 20 meters, how much work was done? (think of a person pushing against a big box to slide it across the floor)

W = 10 N X 20 meters = 200 N*m or 200 Joules

Calculate Work:

W = force * distance

Result: 0.00 Joules or Newton Meters

What is Power?

POWER is work done over a period of time. The unit for power is Watts (W) or Joules/sec

P = Work/time

Example:

If you know the amount of work done and how much time it took, the amount of power can be calculated.

If the job requires 20,000 Joules of work, and it takes 2 days, the amount of power is???

Remember to convert time to seconds: 2 days = 24 hours/1 day X 60 min/1 hour X 60 sec/1 min = 172,800 seconds

Calculate Power:

P = work/time

Result: 0.00 Watts or Joules/second

Bucket challenge description here.

This is our group of teachers last year building the 5 gallon hydroelectric dam to light a diode.

If you were given the following supplies, how could you successfully build a hydroelectric dam?

  1. A five gallon bucket.
  2. A length of PVC pipe (1/2 inch diameter, 18 inches in length but you can shorten)
  3. A hydroelectric generator for the PVC pipe
  4. A 10 watt diode (attached to generator)

Factors Affecting Dam:

Below are some factors that will need to be considered:

  1. What length should you use for the PVC pipe when constructing the dam?
  2. What is the least amount of water you can hold in your resivoir (the bucket) to light your diode when released through the turbine?

Hint: Hydroelectric dams are at most 90% efficient.
